Guard Your HVAC System with a Corrosion Grenade

Living near Florida’s coast can expose your air conditioning system to harsh, corrosive elements—particularly salt spray. Installing a corrosion grenade is a proactive way to protect your investment. This small zinc alloy device acts as a “sacrificial anode,” attracting and absorbing corrosive elements before they can harm critical components of your HVAC system.

By preventing corrosion, you can extend the lifespan of your equipment, optimize its performance, and avoid costly repairs down the road. What is an HVAC corrosion grenade?

A corrosion grenade is a small zinc alloy device that acts as a sacrificial anode, attracting and absorbing corrosive elements before they can damage your HVAC system’s components.

How does a corrosion grenade protect my HVAC system?

It prevents rust and corrosion by sacrificing itself to corrosive elements like salt and moisture. This extends the lifespan of your HVAC unit, especially in coastal areas with high salt exposure.

Who should consider installing a corrosion grenade?

Homeowners near the coast or in high-humidity areas benefit the most from corrosion grenades, as salt air and moisture can accelerate HVAC system wear and deterioration.

How long does a corrosion grenade last?

On average, corrosion grenades last 12 to 24 months before needing replacement. Regular inspections ensure they continue to protect your system effectively.

Can a corrosion grenade improve my system’s efficiency?

Yes! By preventing corrosion on critical components like coils and fins, a corrosion grenade helps maintain peak efficiency and reduces the need for costly repairs.

Is installation complicated?

No, installation is quick and straightforward when done by a professional. Our technicians securely place the corrosion grenade in a strategic location to maximize protection.
