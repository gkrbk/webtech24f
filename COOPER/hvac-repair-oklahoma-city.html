Signs that your HVAC system might need a repair:

  • Warm Air Blowing from Vents: A clear indicator your system isn't cooling properly.
  • Strange Noises: Rattling, banging, squealing, or hissing can signal serious issues.
  • Weak Airflow: Significant decreases in airflow may point to clogged filters, leaks, or other problems.
  • High Energy Bills: A sudden spike in costs without increased usage can mean your unit is working too hard.
  • Inconsistent Cooling: Some rooms being warmer or cooler could indicate duct or airflow concerns.
  • Frequent Cycling: Rapid on-and-off cycles might mean your system is struggling to maintain the set temperature.
  • Water Leaks: Leaks around the unit suggest refrigerant or drainage issues.
  • Burning Smell: Electrical odors must be addressed immediately to prevent damage or danger.
  • Age of the Unit: Systems over 10 years old may need more frequent repairs or a complete replacement.
  • Thermostat Issues: A thermostat that won't respond or maintain temperature could be part of the problem.

What are the most common signs my HVAC system needs repair?

Look for strange noises, weak airflow, unpleasant odors, or a sudden spike in your utility bills. If your system is leaking water, cycling on and off too often, or just not keeping you comfortable, it's best to have it inspected.

How quickly can Cooper Heat & Air respond to repair requests?

We prioritize repair calls and offer same-day service whenever possible, especially for urgent situations. Our goal is to restore your comfort quickly, and our trucks are stocked with common parts to handle most repairs in one visit.

Do you repair all brands and types of HVAC systems?

Yes, our NATE-certified technicians are trained to service and repair all major makes and models. Whether you have a central air conditioner, furnace, heat pump, or ductless unit, we have the expertise to fix it right.

Will I get an upfront estimate before repairs begin?

Absolutely. After our technician diagnoses the issue, we'll explain the problem and provide a clear, flat-rate price for the repair. You approve the cost before any work begins, so there are no surprises on your bill.

When should I repair versus replace my HVAC system?

If your system is newer and the repair cost is reasonable, a repair is usually the best choice. For older systems with recurring issues or a major part failure, replacement can be a better long-term investment. We'll give you an honest assessment to help you decide.

Are your repairs guaranteed?

Yes. We stand behind our work with a comprehensive warranty on parts and labor to ensure your peace of mind. Our focus is on providing a reliable, long-lasting solution, not just a quick fix.
