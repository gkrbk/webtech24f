Common signs your gas furnace needs repair:

  • No Heat or Insufficient Heat: The most obvious sign of a problem, which could stem from several issues.
  • Yellow Pilot Light: A yellow or flickering pilot light can indicate a carbon monoxide leak and requires immediate attention.
  • Unusual Noises: Scraping, banging, or squealing sounds can point to mechanical problems with the blower or other components.
  • Frequent Cycling: If your furnace turns on and off constantly, it may have a clogged filter or a faulty thermostat.
  • Higher Gas Bills: A sudden spike in your energy bills can signal that your furnace is operating inefficiently.

What are the most common gas furnace problems?

Common issues include a furnace that won't turn on, a pilot light that's out, strange noises during operation, frequent cycling, or the blower running continuously. Our technicians can diagnose and resolve all these problems.

Is a yellow pilot light dangerous?

Yes, a pilot light that is yellow or orange instead of blue is a sign of incomplete combustion, which can produce carbon monoxide. If you see this, turn off your furnace and call us for an emergency repair immediately.

How often should I have my gas furnace serviced?

We recommend annual maintenance for your gas furnace, ideally in the fall before the heating season begins. Regular servicing ensures safe operation, maintains efficiency, and can prevent costly breakdowns.

Do you repair all brands of gas furnaces?

Yes, our certified technicians are trained to repair all major brands of gas furnaces. We have the expertise to get your heating system back in working order, no matter the make or model.

Can a furnace repair improve my home's energy efficiency?

Absolutely. Repairing issues like clogged filters, faulty thermostats, or inefficient burners can significantly improve your furnace's performance and lower your monthly gas bills.
