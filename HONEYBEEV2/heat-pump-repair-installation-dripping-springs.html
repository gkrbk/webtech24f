Signs that your heat pump might need a repair:

  • Not Heating or Cooling Properly: The most obvious sign of a problem.
  • Unusual Noises: Grinding, squealing, or rattling sounds can indicate mechanical issues.
  • Increased Energy Bills: A sudden spike suggests your heat pump is losing efficiency.
  • Short Cycling: If the unit turns on and off frequently, it's not completing a full cycle.
  • Ice Buildup: Frost or ice on the coils, especially in winter, can indicate a problem.
  • Reduced Airflow: Weak airflow from your vents can be a sign of a blockage or a failing component.

What is a heat pump and how does it work?

A heat pump is an all-in-one heating and cooling system. In the summer, it functions like an air conditioner, moving heat from inside your home to the outside. In the winter, it reverses the process, extracting heat from the outdoor air and moving it inside to warm your home.

What are the benefits of installing a heat pump?

Heat pumps are highly energy-efficient, which can lead to significant savings on your utility bills. They also provide both heating and cooling in a single unit, simplifying your home's HVAC system. They are also more environmentally friendly than traditional furnaces.

How often does my heat pump need maintenance?

We recommend professional maintenance for your heat pump at least once a year. Regular maintenance ensures your system runs efficiently, helps prevent costly breakdowns, and extends the lifespan of your unit. A spring check-up before the cooling season and a fall check-up before the heating season is ideal.

What is "emergency heat" on my heat pump thermostat?

The emergency heat setting (often labeled "em heat") on your thermostat activates a secondary heat source, usually electric resistance coils. This is meant to be used only in colder temperatures when the heat pump can't efficiently extract enough heat from the outside air, or if the heat pump itself is malfunctioning. It is less efficient and more expensive to run than the standard heat pump setting.

Can a heat pump both heat and cool my home?

Yes, one of the primary advantages of a heat pump is its ability to provide both heating and cooling. In the summer, it extracts heat from inside your home and moves it outside. In the winter, it reverses the process, extracting heat from the outdoor air and moving it inside.

Are heat pumps effective in cold climates?

Modern heat pumps are designed to be effective even in colder climates. Many models can operate efficiently in temperatures well below freezing. For extremely cold weather, they can be paired with a supplementary heating source, such as a furnace, to ensure your home stays warm.

How long do heat pumps typically last?

With proper maintenance, a heat pump can last 15 years or more. Regular service from Honey Bee Heating & Air, LLC helps ensure its longevity and optimal performance.
